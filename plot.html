How Does the Converter Work?

Understanding the conversion between normal and Ramadan schedules.

During Ramadan, working hours are shortened to accommodate fasting and religious observances. The converter uses a linear function to map every point on the normal schedule to its corresponding Ramadan time.

The graph below shows this relationship. The solid line represents the conversion function, while the dashed line (y = x) represents what the schedule would look like with no change. Notice how the conversion line stays below the diagonal — this reflects the compressed Ramadan hours, where an 8:00 AM – 7:30 PM normal day maps to roughly 9:00 AM – 4:20 PM.

The gold dots mark official reference times from the 2026 Ramadan schedule, and the linear function passes through each of them.
